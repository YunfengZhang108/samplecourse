Week 13,14

Applying separation of variables to solve the Laplace equation on a rectangle, a disk, a wedge, outside of a disk. Poisson formula and Poisson kernel. Mean value theorem for harmonic functions.

Week 12

  • Day 1: Fourier sum as the unique best $L^2$-approximation. Bessel's inequality and Parseval's equality.
  • Day 2: The $L^2$-approximation by the partial Fourier sum $\sum_{n=1}^NA_nX_n$ gets better as $N$ increases. The partial Fourier sum $S_N$ for the two orthogonal systems $\{1,\cos\frac{n\pi x}{L},\sin\frac{n\pi x}{L}, x\in[-L,L], n=1,2\ldots\}$ and $\{e^{i\frac{n\pi x}{L}},x\in[-L,L],n=\ldots, -2,-1,0,1,2,\ldots\}$ coincide: $$S_N(x)=\frac{a_0}{2}+\sum_{n=1}^Na_n\cos\frac{n\pi x}{L}+b_n\sin\frac{n\pi x}{L}=\sum_{n=-N}^{N}c_ne^{i\frac{n\pi x}{L}}.$$
  • Day 3: The kernel function $K_N(\theta)=\frac{\sin\left[(N+\frac{1}{2})\pi \theta/L\right]}{\sin\left[\frac{1}{2}\pi \theta/L\right]}$ is not an approximation of the delta function. The Fejér sum $F_N(x)=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}S_N(x)$ and the Fejér kernel $\mathcal{F}_N(\theta)=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}K_N(\theta)$.
